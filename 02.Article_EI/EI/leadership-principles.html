Foundation

The Trust Equation

A framework for building trustworthiness: Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy / Self-Orientation.

C

Credibility

Words we speak; can we be believed? Expertise and honesty in communication.

R

Reliability

Actions and consistency; do we meet expectations? Following through on commitments.

I

Intimacy

Human connection; safety in vulnerability. Building trust through empathy.

S

Self-Orientation

Focus on self vs. others; lower is better. Prioritizing team success over ego.

Core Moves

Emotional Intelligence at Work

Four observable moves: Reading the room, choosing response, influencing weather, caring on purpose. Enhanced with curiosity, silence vs. agreement, engaging introverts, and individuality.

1. Reading the Room — Including Yourself

Awareness of felt emotions in meetings, calls, and internally. Poor decisions often skip this step. Leaders show up with curiosity, asking questions to understand perspectives rather than assuming.

2. Choosing Your Response

The deliberate pause between stimulus and reaction. Leaders are not impassive but deliberate. Distinguish silence from agreement; encourage vocal dissent to avoid groupthink.

3. Influencing the Emotional Weather

Setting the room's climate: lifting energy, cooling tempers, creating safety for good thinking. Engage introverts by providing space for reflection, using written inputs, and valuing silent contributions.

4. Caring on Purpose

Consistent empathy: taking people seriously, their context, constraints, intent. Turns clever leaders into trusted ones. Encourage individuality by celebrating diverse strengths and fostering psychological safety.

Curiosity and Leader Presence

How leaders show up matters. Curiosity drives better understanding, unlearning assumptions, and scaling innovations through boundary-spanning.

Psychological Safety and Empathy

Create conditions for candor. Empathy is non-negotiable; practice it through mobilizing, engaging, and connecting with others.

Advanced EI

Deepening Emotional Intelligence

Incorporating Hogan assessments, HBR insights, and practical techniques for inclusive leadership.

Personality Insights (Hogan)

Use assessments to measure bright side, dark side, and inside of personality for predictive performance and self-awareness.

Engaging Introverts

Provide reflection time, written inputs, one-on-one check-ins, and value silent contributions to harness diverse thinking.

Encouraging Individuality

Celebrate unique strengths, foster psychological safety, and avoid defensive behaviors to promote mattering and retention.

Application

Leadership Principles in Action

Combining Trust Equation and EI for impact: feedback from care, prioritization focus, people-first evaluation.

Feedback from Care & Improvement

360-degree feedback anchored in care, focusing on prioritization clarity and protecting responsibilities, not just cataloging failures.

People as Priority

Roles are demanding; evaluation should improve processes and safeguard contributions, making feedback constructive.
